About Bhopal

The Heart of India

Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, is known as the "City of Lakes" for its various natural and artificial lakes. It is one of the greenest cities in India and is a perfect blend of scenic beauty, old historic monuments, and modern urban planning.

Founded by Raja Bhoj in the 11th century, Bhopal has a rich cultural heritage that reflects the harmony between Hindu and Islamic traditions. The city's architecture, cuisine, and festivals showcase this unique cultural fusion.

★

Rich Cultural Heritage

Home to ancient temples, magnificent mosques, and palaces that showcase a blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture.

★

Natural Beauty

Adorned with beautiful lakes, gardens, and parks that provide a refreshing escape from urban life.

★

Modern Development

A rapidly growing smart city with excellent infrastructure, educational institutions, and technological advancements.

Popular Attractions

Upper Lake (Bada Talab)

One of the largest man-made lakes in Asia, offering boating, water sports, and stunning sunset views.

Taj-ul-Masajid

One of Asia's largest mosques, featuring impressive architecture with pink façades, marble flooring, and a spacious courtyard.

State Museum

Established in 1964, showcases 50,000+ artefacts in a 19th-century Indo-Saracenic building, featuring sculptures, coins, paintings, textiles, weapons, and tribal art from Madhya Pradesh’s rich heritage.

Van Vihar National Park & Zoo

A scenic wildlife sanctuary along the Upper Lake, home to tigers, leopards, bears, deer, and migratory birds in a natural habitat.

Bhimbetka Rock Shelters

UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring prehistoric cave paintings dating back to approximately 30,000 years ago.

Sanchi Stupa

An ancient Buddhist monument built by Emperor Ashoka, known for its intricate carvings and historical significance.
